The Damage panel configures vehicle deformation, detachable parts, and collision response. This enables realistic damage simulation where vehicles visually respond to impacts and parts can break off.

What This Panel Configures

Mesh Deformation

How the vehicle body deforms on impact:

Deformation Radius

How wide an area is affected by impact:

  • Larger radius = more spread deformation
  • Smaller radius = localized dents

Deformation Multiplier

How much the mesh deforms:

  • Higher values = more dramatic deformation
  • Lower values = subtle damage

Deformation Limit

Maximum deformation allowed:

  • Prevents mesh from inverting
  • Keeps damage realistic

Repairability

Whether deformation can be reset:

  • Enable for gameplay repair mechanics
  • Disable for permanent damage

Wheel Damage

How wheels respond to impacts:

Wheel Detachment

Wheels can break off on severe impact:

  • Detachment force threshold
  • Physics for detached wheels
  • Gameplay implications

Wheel Deformation

Wheels can bend from impacts:

  • Affects handling when damaged
  • Visual deformation

Tire Puncture

Tires can be damaged:

  • Affects grip and handling
  • Visual flat tire state

Light Damage

Integration with the Lights panel:

Breakable Lights

Lights turn off when damaged:

  • Per-light configuration
  • Break force threshold
  • Visual damage state

Detachable Parts

Vehicle components that can break off:

Supported Parts:

  • Hood/Bonnet
  • Trunk/Boot
  • Doors (left/right, front/rear)
  • Front bumper
  • Rear bumper
  • Side mirrors
  • Fenders
  • Spoilers/Wings

Part Properties

Each detachable part has:

Strength

How much force breaks the attachment:

  • Higher = harder to detach
  • Lower = easier to break off

Mass

Weight of the detached part:

  • Affects physics when detached
  • Influences momentum transfer

Hinge Point

Where the part attaches:

  • Determines swing direction
  • Affects detachment animation

Break Direction

Which direction detaches the part:

  • Front impact vs rear impact
  • Side impacts

Common Configurations

Realistic Damage 


Configure realistic damage simulation:

- Moderate deformation radius for natural spread

- Progressive deformation that builds up

- All body panels detachable with appropriate strength

- Wheels can detach from severe impacts

- Lights break on impact

- Doors require side impact to detach

- Hood detaches from front impacts

Arcade Damage 


Set up arcade-style damage:

- Minimal mesh deformation (mostly visual, no handling change)

- Parts detach dramatically on any significant impact

- Lower strength values for easy detachment

- Instant repair available

- Exaggerated visual effects

Simulation Damage 


Configure simulation-level damage:

- Accurate deformation based on impact energy

- Realistic part strengths based on real vehicle construction

- Damage affects handling progressively

- Wheels damage affects alignment

- Structural damage possible

- No magical repair

Indestructible Mode 


Make the vehicle immune to damage:

- Disable mesh deformation

- Disable all part detachment

- Disable wheel damage

- Vehicle maintains appearance regardless of impacts

Understanding Deformation

How It Works

When an impact occurs:

  1. Impact point is detected
  2. Force magnitude is calculated
  3. Deformation is applied to nearby vertices
  4. Vertices move inward based on settings

Vertex Movement

Vertices within the deformation radius move:

  • Most movement at impact point
  • Less movement toward edges of radius
  • Limited by deformation limit

Performance Considerations

Mesh deformation is computationally intensive:

  • Lower vertex count = faster deformation
  • Large deformation radius = more vertices affected
  • Consider LOD integration for distant vehicles

Detachable Parts Deep Dive

Part Detection

RCCP identifies parts based on naming conventions:

  • "Hood" or "Bonnet" for front panel
  • "Trunk" or "Boot" for rear panel
  • "Door_FL" for front left door
  • Similar patterns for other parts

Configuring Parts 


Set up detachable hood:

- Strength: 5000N (medium impact detaches)

- Mass: 15kg

- Hinge at rear edge

- Detaches from front impacts

- Physics enabled when detached

Part Behavior When Detached

Detached parts:

  • Become separate physics objects
  • Can collide with environment
  • May bounce or slide
  • Eventually despawn or persist

Partial Detachment

Some parts support partial detachment:

  • Doors swing open before breaking off
  • Hood pops up before flying off
  • Adds realism to damage progression

Damage Zones

Zone Configuration

Different areas can have different properties:

Front Zone

Typically designed for crush zones:

  • Higher deformation allowed
  • Hood detachment area
  • Bumper breakaway

Side Zones

Structural areas:

  • Lower deformation
  • Door hinge points
  • Mirror detachment

Rear Zone

Trunk area:

  • Moderate deformation
  • Trunk detachment
  • Rear bumper breakaway

Per-Zone Settings 


Configure front zone:

- Increased deformation multiplier for crush zone

- Lower strength for bumper detachment

- Headlight damage zone



Configure side zones:

- Reduced deformation for structural integrity

- Door detachment requires strong side impact

- Mirror breaks easily



Configure rear zone:

- Moderate deformation

- Taillight damage zone

- Trunk detaches from rear impacts

Wheel Damage Detail

Detachment

Wheels detach under severe impacts: 


Configure wheel detachment:

- Detachment force: 15000N

- Detached wheels become physics objects

- Vehicle becomes undriveable without wheels

- Visual suspension damage when wheel is gone

Deformation

Wheels bend from impacts: 


Configure wheel deformation:

- Camber changes from side impacts

- Toe changes from front impacts

- Affects handling progressively

- Visual rim damage

Tire Damage

Tires can puncture: 


Configure tire puncture:

- Puncture chance from debris

- Gradual deflation or instant flat

- Handling degrades with flat tire

- Visual flat tire model

Prompt Examples

Basic Setup 


Enable damage system with moderate settings:

- Mesh deforms realistically

- Major panels (hood, trunk, doors) are detachable

- Lights break on direct impact

- Wheels can detach from extreme impacts

Tuning Deformation 


The deformation is too extreme. Reduce the deformation multiplier

by 30% and limit maximum deformation to prevent mesh inversion.

Keep the radius the same for natural spread.

Part Strength Adjustment 


The hood comes off too easily. Increase hood strength to 8000N.

The doors should require a stronger impact - set to 6000N.

Mirrors can stay easy to break at 500N.

Adding New Detachable Part 


Add the front spoiler as a detachable part:

- Strength: 2000N (breaks off easily)

- Mass: 3kg

- Detaches from front impacts

- Physics enabled when detached

Handling Impact 


Configure how damage affects handling:

- Front damage causes steering pull

- Side damage affects tire alignment

- Suspension damage reduces handling precision

- Accumulative damage makes vehicle harder to control

Tips for This Panel

Test with Collisions

After configuration, deliberately crash the vehicle to see results. Use slow-motion to observe deformation.

Balance Realism vs Fun

Too much damage can frustrate players. Find the right balance for your game.

Consider Reset Mechanics

If players can repair, configure repairability. If damage is permanent, balance difficulty accordingly.

Use Appropriate Strengths

Research real vehicle construction for realistic values. Or tune for gameplay feel.

Integrate with Other Systems

Damage affects handling (Behaviors), lights (Lights), and possibly audio. Consider the full picture.

Performance Testing

Test damage with multiple vehicles. Heavy deformation can impact performance.

Visual Feedback

Consider adding particle effects, sounds, and screen shake for impactful damage feedback.
