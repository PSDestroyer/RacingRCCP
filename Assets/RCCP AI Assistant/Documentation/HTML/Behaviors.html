The Behaviors panel focuses on driving dynamics, stability systems, and steering response. Use this panel to define how the vehicle feels to drive and how it responds to driver inputs.

What This Panel Configures

Driving Styles

The overall character of the vehicle's handling:

  • Sporty: Quick responses, minimal assists, driver-focused
  • Comfortable: Smooth transitions, forgiving behavior
  • Racing: Maximum performance, professional-level control
  • Off-road: High travel, stable on uneven terrain
  • Drift: Loose rear end, counter-steer friendly

Stability Systems

Electronic aids that assist the driver:

ABS (Anti-lock Braking System)

Prevents wheel lockup during braking. Configurable parameters:

  • Enable/disable
  • Intervention strength (0-1)
  • Activation threshold

ESP (Electronic Stability Program)

Corrects oversteer and understeer. Configurable parameters:

  • Enable/disable
  • Correction strength
  • Slip angle threshold
  • Brake intervention rate

TCS (Traction Control System)

Limits wheel spin during acceleration. Configurable parameters:

  • Enable/disable
  • Slip limit
  • Power reduction rate
  • Engagement smoothness

Steering Response

How the vehicle responds to steering input:

Steering Angle

Maximum wheel turn angle at standstill.

Speed-Sensitive Steering

Steering multiplier curves that reduce sensitivity at high speeds:

  • X-axis: Vehicle speed (km/h)
  • Y-axis: Steering multiplier (0-1)

Counter-Steer Assistance

Automatic correction during slides:

  • Enable/disable
  • Assistance strength
  • Activation threshold

Driving Limits

Speed Limiter

Maximum vehicle speed enforcement:

  • Enable/disable
  • Speed limit value
  • Enforcement method

Rev Limiter

Engine RPM ceiling behavior:

  • Hard limiter (fuel cut)
  • Soft limiter (gradual reduction)

Common Configurations

Racing Setup 


Create a racing setup with:

- ABS enabled at 0.8 strength for threshold braking

- ESP disabled for maximum driver control

- TCS at 0.3 for corner exit assist

- Quick steering response that reduces by 40% at 200 km/h

- No speed limiter

Drift Setup 


Configure for drifting:

- ABS at 0.5 for brake-initiated slides

- ESP completely disabled

- TCS disabled for unrestricted wheel spin

- Maximum steering angle for deep counter-steer

- Counter-steer assistance at 0.6 strength

Stable Daily Driver 


Set up a stable daily driver:

- ABS at full strength for safe braking

- ESP enabled at 0.7 with early intervention

- TCS enabled at 0.5 to prevent accidental wheel spin

- Progressive steering that becomes very stable at highway speeds

- Speed limiter at 180 km/h

Off-Road Configuration 


Configure for off-road use:

- ABS at 0.4 to allow controlled slides on loose surfaces

- ESP at 0.3 for light correction without fighting terrain

- TCS at 0.4 balanced for traction without over-intervention

- Linear steering response regardless of speed

Understanding Steering Curves

Steering curves are essential for realistic handling. The X-axis represents speed in km/h, and the Y-axis represents a multiplier (0-1) applied to the maximum steering angle.

Example Curve Points

Speed (km/h)MultiplierEffect
01.0Full steering at standstill
500.880% steering at city speeds
1000.550% steering at highway speeds
2000.330% steering at high speeds

Why This Matters

At high speeds:

  • Full steering would cause immediate loss of control
  • Reduced steering makes the vehicle stable
  • Real cars have this effect mechanically

The curve should:

  • Start at 1.0 for low speeds
  • Decrease progressively
  • Level off at high speeds

Describing Curves to the AI

Instead of specifying exact curve points, describe the behavior: 


Make steering very responsive at parking speeds but reduce to about

30% effectiveness at highway speeds. The transition should be gradual

and smooth, not abrupt.

Stability System Deep Dive

ABS Configuration

When to enable:

  • Road vehicles for consistent braking
  • Racing where threshold braking is allowed
  • Wet conditions

When to reduce/disable:

  • Off-road for controlled slides
  • Drift vehicles for brake-initiated techniques
  • Realistic simulations where driver skill matters

Strength guidelines:

  • 1.0: Maximum intervention, wheel never locks
  • 0.7-0.8: Strong assist, brief lockups possible
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate, requires driver attention
  • 0.1-0.3: Light assist, mostly driver controlled
  • 0.0: Disabled

ESP Configuration

When to enable:

  • Novice-friendly vehicles
  • High-powered vehicles
  • Adverse conditions

When to reduce/disable:

  • Drift vehicles (ESP fights sliding)
  • Professional racing simulations
  • When deliberate oversteer is desired

Strength guidelines:

  • 1.0: Very aggressive correction
  • 0.6-0.8: Strong but allows minor slides
  • 0.3-0.5: Moderate, catches big slides only
  • 0.1-0.2: Light safety net
  • 0.0: Disabled

TCS Configuration

When to enable:

  • Powerful vehicles
  • Slippery conditions
  • Novice-friendly setups

When to reduce/disable:

  • Drift vehicles
  • When wheel spin is desired for show
  • Rally/off-road for terrain adaptation

Strength guidelines:

  • 1.0: No wheel spin possible
  • 0.6-0.8: Minimal slip allowed
  • 0.3-0.5: Moderate slip before intervention
  • 0.1-0.2: Only prevents extreme spin
  • 0.0: Disabled

Counter-Steer Assistance

This feature helps maintain control during slides by automatically applying corrective steering.

How It Works

  1. Vehicle begins to slide (rear stepping out)
  2. System detects slip angle
  3. Steering is automatically adjusted toward the slide direction
  4. Driver maintains control with less effort

When to Enable

  • Drift vehicles (makes drifting easier)
  • Arcade-style handling
  • Novice-friendly configurations
  • When natural drifting feel is desired

When to Disable

  • Simulation-focused vehicles
  • Professional driving experience
  • When maximum driver control is preferred

Strength Guidelines

  • 1.0: Very strong assistance (almost automatic)
  • 0.5-0.7: Helpful without taking over
  • 0.2-0.4: Subtle assistance
  • 0.0: Disabled (pure driver control)

Preset Examples

"Track Day" 


Sharp steering response at low speeds, stabilizing at high speeds.

Strong ABS for consistent braking. ESP disabled for driver control.

Light TCS for corner exit traction. No speed limiter.

"Sunday Drive" 


Smooth, predictable steering at all speeds. Full ABS for safety.

Moderate ESP for stability. TCS enabled to prevent wheel spin.

Speed limiter at 200 km/h.

"Rally Stage" 


Quick steering maintained even at speed for rapid corrections.

Reduced ABS for controlled slides on gravel. Light ESP for

extreme situations only. TCS off for natural terrain adaptation.

"Arcade Fun" 


Very responsive steering. Moderate ABS. ESP at 0.5 for

catchable slides. TCS off. Strong counter-steer assistance

for easy drifting.

Tips for This Panel

Start with Stability Systems

Get the electronic aids right first - they have the biggest impact on feel.

Test at Multiple Speeds

Steering curves affect different speed ranges. Test parking, city, and highway speeds.

Balance Related Settings

ESP and TCS interact. Very strong ESP with no TCS can feel inconsistent.

Consider the Whole Package

Behaviors affect how power delivery and suspension feel. Tune iteratively.

Use Descriptive Language

Instead of numbers, describe feelings: "catches slides but allows some fun."
